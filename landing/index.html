Capture over kill

A wrecked drone tells you nothing.
A captured one tells you everything.

Every other counter-UAS answer ends in a fireball or a jammed band — and both discard the most valuable thing in the sky: the drone itself. We take it whole. The airframe flies again. Its memory — flight logs, waypoints, payload imagery, firmware — survives, bit for bit, back to the launch point. The decision to engage stays human.

The engagement

Detect. Commit.
Capture. Recover.

Onboard EO/IR sensors hold the track and feed a stabilized picture to the operator. A human classifies the contact and authorizes the intercept. A software-aimed launcher places the net; a cinch line draws the catch closed. The interceptor carries it home — intact.

EO·IR operator feed

Built for the operator,
not around them.

A single, legible cockpit: a live EO/IR feed, a heads-up state overlay, and a control scheme tuned for the seconds that matter. Targeting is software-assisted. The trigger is not. Fire, cinch and release stay interlocked behind an armed state — never automatic.

Exploitation — ForensIQ-1

Every capture
is a confession.

A recovered drone is a sealed record. ForensIQ-1 is a write-blocked, read-only exploitation appliance that turns recovered storage into a signed chain-of-custody report — and, where the data allows, pins the geolocated launch point of the hostile aircraft.
