Capture over kill

A wrecked drone tells you nothing.
A captured one tells you everything.

A missile scatters debris over whatever is below and burns the evidence of who sent it. A jammer takes down your own net along with theirs. We take the airframe whole — it flies again — and its memory survives bit for bit: flight logs, waypoints, payload imagery, firmware, all the way back to whoever launched it.

The engagement — two nets, one turret

Detect. Commit.
Capture or neutralise.

A recon drone is taken in the close-in capture net and cinched shut — intact, on its way home for exploitation. A kamikaze drone gets the large stand-off net from outside its blast radius; the tether is cut and the whole catch is dumped over open ground. Their attack drone never touches yours.
