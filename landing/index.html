01 / Capture over kill

A wrecked drone tells you nothing. A captured one tells you everything.

Every other counter-UAS answer ends in a fireball or a jammed band — and both throw away the most valuable thing in the sky: the drone itself.

The airframe is an asset

A netted drone lands whole — motors, optics and frame intact. Recover it, refly it, or strip it for parts.

The memory is intelligence

Flight logs, waypoints, payload imagery, firmware. A missile vaporises it; capture preserves it, bit for bit, back to the launch point.

The decision stays human

No autonomy decides who gets engaged. An operator classifies the contact and commits from the cockpit. Never automatic.

02 / The engagement

Detect. Commit. Capture. Recover.

  1. 01

    Acquire the track

    Onboard EO/IR sensors, fused with RTK-grade navigation, hold the target and feed a stabilized picture to the operator.

  2. 02

    Operator commits

    A human classifies the contact and authorizes the intercept. Arm, fire, cinch and release are interlocked — never automatic.

  3. 03

    Net the target

    A software-aimed launcher places the net on the moving drone; a cinch line draws the catch closed against the airframe.

  4. 04

    Bring it home intact

    The interceptor carries the catch back to base, where the airframe is recovered and its storage handed to exploitation.

03 / EO·IR sensor feed

Built for the operator, not around them.

The ground station is a single, legible cockpit: a live EO/IR feed, a heads-up state overlay, and a control scheme tuned for the seconds that matter. Targeting is software-assisted; the trigger is not.

  • Flight — pitch, roll, yaw and throttle on a low-latency manual loop
  • Net aim — pan/tilt the launcher against the tracked target
  • Interlocks — fire, cinch and release require an armed state
  • Recovery — return-to-home and emergency stop always within reach

04 / Exploitation — ForensIQ-1

Every capture is a confession.

A recovered drone is a sealed record. ForensIQ-1 is a write-blocked, read-only exploitation appliance that turns recovered storage into a chain-of-custody report — and, where the data allows, pins the geolocated launch point of the hostile aircraft.

  1. 01

    Intake

    Recovered media is logged, photographed and sealed under custody.

  2. 02

    Image & hash

    Write-blocked copy, SHA-256 verified bit for bit.

  3. 03

    Reconstruct

    Flight logs parsed into a full trajectory and payload timeline.

  4. 04

    Geolocate

    Trajectory walked back to an estimated launch origin with error bounds.

  5. 05

    Report

    Signed PDF and field printout with a complete custody trail.

ForensIQ-1 produces intelligence for the commander. It is an analysis tool — never an instruction to act.
