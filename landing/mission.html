Mission

Turn their drones into your intelligence.

The cheap drone is winning because it is disposable. We make it expensive again — by taking it whole and reading everything it carried.

The problem

The modern battlefield is saturated with small unmanned aircraft. Reconnaissance drones map positions and call in fire; one-way attack drones dive into vehicles and people. They cost a few hundred dollars and they are meant to be thrown away — so every counter answer that destroys them on contact is playing the loser's side of the cost curve, and worse, it incinerates the one thing each drone is carrying: a record of who sent it, from where, and to hit what.

Shooting drones down rains debris on whatever is below and erases the evidence. Jamming the spectrum is indiscriminate and takes your own links down with the threat. Both throw away the intelligence and leave the operator who launched the drone completely untouched, free to launch the next one.

Our answer

TaloNet flies a larger mothership that nets a hostile drone out of the air and brings it back intact. A reconnaissance drone comes home whole. A kamikaze drone is fouled by a large net from outside its blast radius, cut loose, and dropped on open ground — the interceptor never touches the warhead.

Then the real work starts. A captured drone is a sealed confession. We read its memory down to the launch point, draw the operating-radius ring around the base it flew from, recover the planned route to its next target, and attribute its firmware and hardware. The enemy's reconnaissance becomes your targeting data. One catch can compromise the operation that sent it.

What we believe

  • Capture beats kill. A wreck is a cost; a capture is a lead. Recovery is the only counter-UAS outcome that grows your intelligence instead of destroying it.
  • The operator owns the engagement. Software finds and aims; a human commits. That is faster than autonomy and keeps accountability where it belongs.
  • Build it so it actually flies. Real airframe, real flight controller, real MAVLink, real forensics — traced from a bill of materials and a parametric model to a working cockpit. Not a render.
  • Earn the asymmetry. Make the disposable drone a liability to the side that launched it.

Where we are going

An interceptor that recovers and exploits faster than the adversary can relaunch — fielded as a system any qualified operator can build, run and trust, from the airframe to the field forensic appliance. We are looking for evaluation and integration partners who own airspace they need to take back.
